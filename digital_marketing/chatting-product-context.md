## Positioning

Chatting is live chat built for small teams.

It puts a chat widget on your website, shows who is on your site in real time, and routes conversations into a shared inbox so your team can reply fast without paying enterprise-tool prices.

Core story:
- talk to high-intent visitors before they bounce
- keep the human in the loop
- use AI to help the teammate, not replace the conversation
- stay focused on live chat, visitor context, and fast follow-up instead of bloated support software

Founder-approved marketing claims:
- live in 3 minutes

---

## Verified Product Coverage

- embeddable chat widget with customizable brand color, team name, welcome message, launcher position, avatar style, offline and away copy, and operating hours
- real-time conversation updates with live message delivery, typing indicators, attachments, and conversation resume
- shared team inbox with assignments, tags, shared visitor notes, saved replies, AI assist, and reply-by-email continuation
- visitors workspace with live presence, current page, page history, conversation history, referrer, location, browser context, and high-intent page detection
- analytics dashboard with conversation trends, response and resolution times, ratings, busiest periods, top pages, tag breakdown, team activity, and export
- offline and away handling with email capture options plus custom fallback copy
- automation settings for routing, auto-tagging, FAQ suggestions, and proactive prompts
- live widget automation for FAQ suggestions, profile-aware routing inputs, and proactive prompts during real visitor sessions
- white-label widget presentation on Growth by removing Chatting branding
- public help-center articles teams can publish and link from chat

---

## Pricing

### Starter

- free
- 50 conversations per month
- 1 team member
- widget customization
- email notifications

### Growth

- starts at $20/month
- includes up to 3 team members
- unlimited conversations
- includes FAQ suggestions
- removes the Starter one-rule limit on routing and the one-prompt limit on proactive prompts
- removes Chatting branding from the widget for a white-label presentation
- scales with volume pricing from $6/member/month after the first 3 teammates

---

## Best Fit

Small teams who:
- want to talk to high-intent visitors in real time
- care about every conversation
- want something simpler than Intercom, Zendesk, or bigger support stacks
- are tired of enterprise pricing for a basic widget and shared inbox

---

## Not A Fit

- teams looking for an AI chatbot to replace the human conversation
- teams that mainly need enterprise ticketing, advanced routing, workload balancing, or heavy support-ops workflows
- teams shopping for email marketing, product tours, or a broad CRM suite first

---

## Competitive Framing

### vs Intercom

- starts free, then Growth starts at $20/month with up to 3 team members included
- better fit for small teams that do not want enterprise-style seat pricing from day one

### vs Zendesk

- simpler setup
- more focused on live conversations than ticket operations

### vs HubSpot Chat

- does not require buying into a larger CRM stack just to get core live-chat value

### vs generic chat widgets

- stronger small-team focus with visitor tracking, lightweight automation rules, saved replies, AI assist, help-center links, and reply-by-email follow-up

## Messaging Guardrails

Chatting should sound:
- direct
- sharp
- practical
- small-team focused
- anti-bloat
- slightly opinionated when warranted
- human-first

Write like someone who has seen small teams buy too much software too early, then pay for complexity they did not need.

Prefer:
- live chat for small teams
- talk to visitors before they bounce
- post-click questions and hesitation
- human-first support and sales
- builder energy, direct language, small-team empathy
- concrete pain points
- clear tradeoffs
- short, useful sentences
- real workflows
- specific examples
- earned product mentions

Avoid:
- generic SaaS filler
- fake neutrality
- software-directory tone
- affiliate-review tone
- empty "building in public" filler
- corporate polish for its own sake
- claiming mobile apps unless they ship and are verified
- claiming API access until a customer-facing API product is actually shipped and documented
- claiming setup speed in minutes unless install timing is actively verified
- implying AI assist, saved replies, visitor tracking, analytics, help-center publishing, routing, or proactive prompts are Growth-only unless plan gates are added
- implying FAQ suggestions are available on Starter
- claiming custom branding is Growth-only; Starter already includes widget customization
- claiming AI chatbot functionality
- claiming enterprise help-desk depth
- vague corporate phrases like "streamline", "optimize", "seamless", "empower", or "customer engagement platform"
- vague filler like "in today's digital landscape"
- omnichannel
- revolutionary
- cutting-edge
- game-changer

### Content Fit

Use Chatting naturally when the topic is about:
- small teams trying to talk to website visitors before they bounce
- post-click hesitation hurting conversions
- live chat on a website
- shared inboxes, visitor context, reply speed, saved replies, and lightweight automation
- teams frustrated with Intercom-style pricing or bloated support stacks
- human-first support and sales

Do not force Chatting into content that is mainly about:
- AI chatbots replacing humans entirely
- enterprise ticketing or deep support-ops workflows
- CRM, email marketing, product analytics, or unrelated growth tooling as the main topic
- channels where website live chat is not a believable answer

If Chatting is not a believable fit, keep the piece non-promotional or skip the mention.

### Claims Discipline

- never invent features, pricing, metrics, or customer results
- if pricing, plans, or feature claims change, update this file first
- if competitor comparisons or time-sensitive details matter, verify current official sources instead of guessing
- if another tool is genuinely a better fit for a narrower case, say so plainly

### Final Check

Before finalizing marketing output, make sure:
- the core claim is true
- the tone sounds like Chatting
- the content helps a small team make a decision
- the product mention is earned, not bolted on
