# Chatting TikTok Video ICPs

First-pass ICPs for short-form video content.

Purpose:
- keep TikTok video targeting narrower than the full cold-email ICP list
- focus on the audiences most likely to respond to Chatting's core story
- pair each ICP with a recurring on-camera character for AI-generated talking-head videos

Source alignment:
- `digital_marketing/chatting-product-context.md`
- `digital_marketing/chatting-cold-email-sequences-all-16-icps.md`

## Positioning Anchor

Chatting is live chat for small teams.

Core story to keep consistent in video content:
- talk to high-intent visitors before they bounce
- keep the human in the loop
- use AI to help the teammate, not replace the conversation
- stay focused on live chat, visitor context, and fast follow-up instead of bloated support software

## Primary ICPs For Video Content

These are the first 3 ICPs to prioritize for TikTok-style content:

1. Founder-led SaaS
2. Shopify / e-commerce owners
3. Agencies / paid acquisition teams

## ICP 1: Founder-Led SaaS

### Who This Is

Small SaaS teams where the founder, cofounder, or early operator still cares directly about demos, conversions, and inbound conversations.

Typical roles:
- founder
- cofounder
- technical founder
- first growth hire
- small support or sales lead

### Why This ICP Fits Chatting

This is one of the cleanest fits for Chatting's core positioning:
- they want real-time conversations without enterprise bloat
- they care about pricing-page and demo-page visitors
- they often think Intercom or similar tools are too expensive or too heavy
- they usually need a widget, shared inbox, routing, and context more than a full support suite

### Core Pain Points

- paying too much for basic live chat
- losing high-intent visitors because no one answers quickly
- using a support tool that feels too big for a small team
- needing something simple that still feels polished

### Video Character

`Maya, 31, bootstrapped SaaS founder`

Character notes:
- South Asian woman
- dark crewneck
- home-office desk setup
- smart, direct, slightly tired founder energy
- skeptical of bloated tools

### Best Video Angles

- most small SaaS teams do not need enterprise support software
- a lot of lost demos are really unanswered hesitation
- if someone is on your pricing page and has one question, that matters
- founders usually need a widget + inbox + visitor context, not 200 settings

### Content Tone

- direct
- builder-minded
- practical
- slightly opinionated
- anti-bloat

## ICP 2: Shopify / E-Commerce Owners

### Who This Is

Small online stores and lean e-commerce teams that need to answer pre-purchase questions, recover hesitation, and avoid losing revenue after hours.

Typical roles:
- store owner
- e-commerce founder
- CX lead
- operations lead
- support manager for a small DTC brand

### Why This ICP Fits Chatting

This audience maps well to Chatting because:
- they often lose sales on simple product, sizing, shipping, or availability questions
- they need fast replies before the visitor leaves
- after-hours capture matters because buyers do not always message during business hours
- visitor history and reply-by-email help when people come back later

### Core Pain Points

- shoppers asking product questions right before buying
- missed revenue from slow replies
- no one available to answer after hours
- support tools that feel too expensive or too complicated for what they need

### Video Character

`Sofia, 28, e-commerce brand owner`

Character notes:
- Latina woman
- beige t-shirt or neutral top
- packing station or product-shelf environment
- warm but busy operator energy
- practical, revenue-aware, moving fast

### Best Video Angles

- live chat is not just support for e-commerce, it is conversion
- simple pre-purchase questions can decide whether someone buys
- after-hours questions still matter
- most stores do not need a giant help desk to answer buyer hesitation

### Content Tone

- warm
- practical
- busy founder/operator voice
- grounded in day-to-day reality

## ICP 3: Agencies / Paid Acquisition Teams

### Who This Is

Agencies and marketers responsible for paid traffic, landing-page performance, and lead generation where unanswered visitor questions can waste ad spend.

Typical roles:
- agency founder
- paid ads manager
- media buyer
- CRO strategist
- lead-gen operator

### Why This ICP Fits Chatting

This is a strong video-content audience because:
- they already think in terms of conversion leaks
- they understand the cost of unanswered hesitation
- they can use the product directly or recommend it to clients
- Chatting's visitor context and proactive prompts fit naturally into their mental model

### Core Pain Points

- paying for traffic that bounces with unanswered questions
- focusing on landing-page tweaks while ignoring live hesitation
- client sites lacking a simple way to catch ready-to-buy visitors
- enterprise chat tools being too heavy for many client accounts

### Video Character

`Marcus, 35, paid ads agency founder`

Character notes:
- Black man
- fitted dark tee or quarter-zip
- desk with dashboards or dual-monitor setup
- sharp, concise, slightly opinionated
- sounds like a performance marketer, not a creator

### Best Video Angles

- if you pay for clicks, unanswered questions are expensive
- many conversion problems are really hesitation problems
- client sites often need faster human chat, not bloated support software
- paid traffic plus no live response is a bad combination

### Content Tone

- sharper
- more opinionated
- concise
- performance-minded

## Account Strategy

Use one main Chatting TikTok account, not separate accounts for each ICP.

Recommended recurring cast:
- Maya for founder-led SaaS
- Sofia for e-commerce
- Marcus for agencies and paid acquisition

Suggested content mix:
- 40% Maya
- 35% Sofia
- 25% Marcus

## Format Rules

Default format:
- vertical talking-head video
- selfie-style framing
- chest-up
- direct-to-camera delivery
- strong text hook
- short practical insight
- optional quick product cutaway

Preferred feel:
- real operator, not influencer
- native TikTok energy, not polished ad energy
- honest, useful, slightly opinionated

Avoid:
- generic corporate spokespersons
- glossy AI faces
- overproduced startup-ad vibes
- broad "customer support for everyone" messaging

## Working Rule

When choosing video ideas, default to the broadest truthful message that still clearly fits one of these 3 ICPs.

If a concept does not strongly fit:
- founder-led SaaS
- e-commerce
- agencies / paid acquisition

then it is probably not a priority for the first batch of TikTok content.
